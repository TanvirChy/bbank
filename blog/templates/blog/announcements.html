Turna Nishita crashes into Udayan Express; over 70 injured; death toll may rise; rail links restored after nearly eight hours; three railway staffs suspended; five probe bodies formed

Turna Nishita crashes into Udayan Express; over 70 injured; death toll may rise; rail links restored after nearly eight hours; three railway staffs suspended; five probe bodies formed

In the worst railway disaster over the past decade, 16 people including five children were killed in a collision between two intercity trains in Kasba upazila of Brahmanbaria early Tuesday.

Dhaka-bound Turna Nishita, coming from Chittagong, crashed into Chittagong-bound Udayan Express, coming from Sylhet, near Mondobagh Railway Station around 2:45am, according to railway and district administration officials. The crash threw at least four bogies of Udayan Express off the tracks and left them in a mangled state, leading to the casualties. At least 200ft stretch of railway tracks was also damaged.
